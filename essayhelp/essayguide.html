Essay Guide

Before you start your essay you need to consider the following things:

  • Formatting
  • Conventions
  • Organization
  • Sentence Fluency
  • Voice
  • Vocabulary
These factors can make your essay stand out a lot. Let's go over each one.

Formatting: Formatting might change based on what you are doing, but in this circumstance, you would follow the MLA (Modern Language Association) guidelines for essays. You must first create a MLA heading. Here is the format:

(Your name here)
(Your teacher's name here)
(Your course here), P(Your period here)
(Today's date here)

You also generate your MLA heading here
Your MLA essay must be double-spaced and be in a legible font like Times New Roman. For more information, you can vist this resource. Another thing that you should add when citing sources is to use the MLA citation format. You can input a link at this resource and it will automatically format the MLA citation for you.


Conventions: Conventions are just basic rules of writing in English like spelling, punctuation, etc. Conventions may be basic, but they can be some of the main reasons that people make mistakes on essays. DO NOT UNDERESTIMATE THE IMPORTANCE OF CONVENTIONS!

Organization: When organizing your essay, you should try to split up your broad ideas into paragraphs and smallers ideas into sentences. For example, in a persuasive or argumentative essay, you might put each reason into each paragraph.

Sentence Fluency: Sentence fluency is very important for the reader of your essay. Short, choppy sentences are not easy to read. Instead of using multiple simple sentences about one topic, use a complex, compound, or compound-complex sentence. For more information about types of sentences, look here.

Voice: In any kind of formal essay, there should be voice. However, at the same time, you shouldn't have too much voice to the point that the essay loses its formality. Voice is how you write uniquely from others. This is why certain advanced readers can tell the difference of two authors based on their writing. Voice makes your paper stand out from others. It isn't something that you can force. You just do it. Unfortunately, adding too much voice might end up making your paper informal, so you should also remember to maintain formality.

Vocabulary: Vocabulary is probably the most overthought of when writing an essay. Although it is really important, make sure to think about the rest of the factors above. The best way to enhance your vocabulary is to use a thesaurus. Merriam Webster and Thesaurus.com are common, useful online resources students can use.


A great website that will check your essay for grammatical errors is EasyBib.

Good luck on your essay!
